About

Curious by nature,
precise by practice.

I grew up in Jeddah, spent years in Lahore, and somehow ended up in Helsinki — four years in and still surprised by the winters. Three very different cities, three very different ways of seeing the world. That kind of upbringing leaves a mark: I am genuinely comfortable with new environments, unfamiliar teams, and people who think nothing like me. I adapt quickly without losing my own point of view.

My work is about helping companies unblock AI: figuring out where it actually creates value, designing systems around it, and making sure what gets built ships and works in the real world. I care a lot about meaning in what I do. If I cannot believe in what I am building, I am genuinely not the right person for it.

I am curious about a lot of things and get bored easily by repetition — which probably explains why I ended up in AI. I like problems that have not been solved yet. I think humor is underrated as a sign of intelligence. And I would rather have one honest conversation than ten polished ones.

Outside of work I read and listen to a lot about psychology and philosophy — the kind that helps you understand people, not just impress them at dinner parties. I travel whenever I get the chance, mostly to places that feel genuinely unfamiliar.
